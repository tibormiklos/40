I'm 40. I'm going for a walk.

Come and hike around the Isle of Man with me.

30 September — 5 October 2018

Hello friends, I'm turning 40 this year and I thought this would be a great opportunity to take some time off and go for a long walk around this beautiful island.

It's a funny thing turning 40 and I see different people handling getting older differently around me. Through taking time off to do something slow, basic and offline I'm hoping to suck out the marrow of life reconnect with friends, spend some quality time out of doors and explore the native landscape.

The plan

The plan is to circumnavigate the Isle of Man, following the Rad Ny Foillan ('way of the gull'/manx coastal footpath). I'm counting on balmy indian summer-esque weather over the week, but with it being the Isle of Man, we could have gale force winds and hail one minute and beautiful sunshine the next.

The below route is based on the one described on the IOM government website, except that we're doing it anti-clockwise, starting in Laxey and heading North towards the Point of Ayre.

There are bed and breakfasts available in most of the towns the route takes in and while I'm keen to spend a few nights camping, I'm not planning on making this too hard for myself.

There are also pubs and shops, so I'm only taking bare essentials, trying to keep it light to give us the option of running a few sections.

I think it's going to be fun and I hope you can make it.
